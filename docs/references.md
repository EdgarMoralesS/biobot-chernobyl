## Plant motion and bioelectronic control

- Li, W. et al. (2021). **An on-demand plant-based actuator created using conformable electrodes.** *Nature Electronics* 4, 134–142. [DOI: 10.1038/s41928-020-00530-4](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41928-020-00530-4) — NTU Singapore; senior author Xiaodong Chen. (Not to be confused with the accompanying News & Views piece, *"A Venus-flytrap-based actuator."*)
- Huang, X. et al. (2023). **Plant electrophysiology with conformable organic electronics: Deciphering the propagation of Venus flytrap action potentials.** *Science Advances* 9, eadh4443. [DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.adh4443](https://www.science.org/doi/abs/10.1126/sciadv.adh4443) — Linköping University (Stavrinidou group) with Columbia University.
- Huang, S. et al. / Hedrich, R. (2021). **Optogenetic control of the guard cell membrane potential and stomatal movement by the light-gated anion channel GtACR1.** *Science Advances* 7, eabg4619. [DOI: 10.1126/sciadv.abg4619](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.abg4619) — JMU Würzburg.
- Sareen, H. & Maes, P. **Cyborg Botany / Elowan** — MIT Media Lab, Fluid Interfaces. [Project page](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/elowan-a-plant-robot-hybrid/overview/). *Note: Elowan is plant→robot (the plant's signals drive a robot). The reverse direction — software → electrode → plant movement — is described in this repo as "Phytoactuators"; we have not been able to anchor that specific project name to a primary source. Treat the direction of control as verified only for Elowan.*
- Guerra, S., Castiello, U. et al. **3D kinematic analysis of movement in pea plants (*Pisum sativum*).** See *"Can Plants Move Like Animals? A Three-Dimensional Stereovision Analysis of Movement in Plants"* ([PMC8300309](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8300309/)) and *"Motor cognition in plants: from thought to real experiments,"* *Theoretical and Experimental Plant Physiology* (2023) ([PMC7616355](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7616355/)).

## Plant electrophysiology under radiation

- Grinberg, M., Ivanova, A., Nemtsova, Y., Pirogova, P., Skamnitskiy, D. & Vodeneev, V. (2026). **Comparative radiosensitivity of morphometric, physiological and signaling parameters in wheat plants.** *International Journal of Radiation Biology*, online 23 March 2026. [DOI: 10.1080/09553002.2026.2645916](https://doi.org/10.1080/09553002.2026.2645916) — **The load-bearing citation for this repo.** Electrical signals are the *most* radiosensitive parameter measured, responding at 25 Gy where growth and photosynthesis only respond at 50–75 Gy.
- Grinberg, M.A. et al. (2025). **Effect of Increased Ionizing Radiation and Near-Null Magnetic Field on Electrical Signals of Plants.** *Radiophysics and Quantum Electronics* 67(10), 788–798. [DOI: 10.1007/s11141-025-10418-y](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11141-025-10418-y)
- Grinberg, M.A. & Vodeneev, V. (2025). **The role of signaling systems of plant in responding to key astrophysical factors: increased ionizing radiation, near-null magnetic field and microgravity.** *Planta* 261, art. 31. [DOI: 10.1007/s00425-025-04610-7](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00425-025-04610-7)
- (2024). **Analysis of Molecular Mechanisms of Chronic Irradiation Effects on Electrical Signals in Wheat Plants.** *Biochemistry (Moscow), Supplement Series A: Membrane and Cell Biology.* [DOI: 10.1134/S1990747824700053](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1134/S1990747824700053) — Source of the 31.3 μGy/h dose rate and the SKOR potassium-channel finding.

## Plants and radiation, generally

- Doonan, J.H. & Sablowski, R. (2010). **Walls around tumours — why plants do not develop cancer.** *Nature Reviews Cancer* 10, 794–802. [DOI: 10.1038/nrc2942](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrc2942) — The primary source for the rigid-cell-wall / no-metastasis mechanism.
- Kovalchuk, I. et al. (2004). **Molecular Aspects of Plant Adaptation to Life in the Chernobyl Zone.** *Plant Physiology* 135(1), 357–363. [PMC429389](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC429389/) — *Arabidopsis*, not conifers; the mechanism reported is DNA hypermethylation and genome stability, not upregulated DNA repair.
- Geras'kin, S. et al. (2019). **The response profile to chronic radiation exposure based on the transcriptome analysis of Scots pine from Chernobyl affected zone.** *Environmental Pollution.* [PubMed 31035144](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31035144/)
- Geras'kin, S. et al. (2017). **Radiation exposure in the remote period after the Chernobyl accident caused oxidative stress and genetic effects in Scots pine populations.** [PMC5320440](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5320440/) — Note that *Pinus sylvestris* is an ICRP reference organism precisely because it is **highly radiosensitive**. See [the caveat in §2](02-plants-under-radiation.md).
- Dadachova, E. et al. (2007). **Ionizing radiation changes the electronic properties of melanin and enhances the growth of melanized fungi.** *PLoS ONE* 2(5), e457. [DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0000457](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0000457) — The "radiotrophic fungi" work. Energy *harvesting* from gamma remains a hypothesis, not an established mechanism.

## Robots and radiation

- Zhang, K., Hutson, C., Knighton, J., Guido, H. & Scott, T.B. (2020). **Radiation Tolerance Testing Methodology of Robotic Manipulator Prior to Nuclear Waste Handling.** *Frontiers in Robotics and AI* 7, 6. [DOI: 10.3389/frobt.2020.00006](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/frobt.2020.00006/full) — Source of the KUKA LBR800 figure (164.55 ± 1.09 Gy, failure of an optical encoder under a 20 TBq Co-60 source). **Read the conclusion, not just the number: the authors judge the arm to have shown *significant* radiation tolerance and to be potentially viable for nuclear waste work.**
- IEEE Spectrum (2 April 2026). **Radiation-Hardened Wi-Fi for Robotics in Nuclear Industry.** [spectrum.ieee.org](https://spectrum.ieee.org/robotics-in-nuclear-industry) — Source of the space (100–300 Gy / 3 yr) vs. reactor (>500 kGy / 6 mo) dose comparison.
- IEEE Spectrum (23 November 2020). **Boston Dynamics' Spot Is Helping Chernobyl Move Towards Safe Decommissioning.** [spectrum.ieee.org](https://spectrum.ieee.org/boston-dynamics-spot-chernobyl) — A 2020 trial deployment, not an ongoing operation.
- Magics Technologies — radiation-hardened ASICs for ITER robotics. [magics.tech](https://www.magics.tech/about) — KU Leuven / SCK CEN spin-off; supplied chips for ITER robotics; rated to ~1 MGy (100 Mrad) TID.
- Hackaday (22 June 2023). **The Many Robots That Ventured Into The Chernobyl NPP #4 Reactor.** [hackaday.com](https://hackaday.com/2023/06/22/the-many-robots-that-ventured-into-the-chernobyl-npp-4-reactor/)
- Phys.org / AFP (Feb 2017). **"Scorpion" robot mission inside Fukushima reactor aborted.** [phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2017-02-scorpion-robot-mission-fukushima-reactor.html) — Source of the 650 Sv/h estimate, the 1,000 Sv camera tolerance, and the two-hours-into-a-ten-hour-mission failure.
- Phys.org / AP (July 2017). **Possible melted fuel seen for first time at Fukushima plant.** [phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2017-07-fuel-fukushima.html) — The "Little Sunfish" (Toshiba + IRID) Unit 3 survey.
- Legasov's remark ("*where there was very high radiation, the robot ceased to be a robot — the electronics quit working*") and the figure of 3,828 roof "bio-robots" are widely documented in the Chernobyl liquidator literature; see [Chernobyl liquidators (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chernobyl_liquidators) for entry points into the primary Soviet sources.
