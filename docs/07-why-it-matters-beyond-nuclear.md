# 7. Why This Matters Beyond Nuclear

[← Back to README](../README.md) · [← Previous: Related work](06-related-work.md)

The semiconductor vulnerability that dooms electronic robots in Chernobyl and Fukushima isn't unique to nuclear-accident sites. The same failure mode — ionizing radiation destroying digital logic — shows up anywhere electronics face doses well beyond terrestrial norms:

- **Deep space exploration** — cosmic radiation accumulates over mission lifetimes with no possibility of on-site repair.
- **Jupiter / Europa missions** — Jupiter's radiation belts are among the most hostile environments in the solar system for electronics.
- **Solar flare events** — can disrupt or damage satellite and ground-based electronics on short notice.
- **Future fusion reactor maintenance** — maintenance robotics inside a fusion reactor face a similar dose environment to fission decommissioning work.
- **Nuclear waste repository monitoring** — some repositories need monitoring on century-long timescales, far past the point where conventional electronics can be maintained or replaced.

If a biological approach to actuation and sensing can be made to work in a radiation environment, it's not a one-off fix for reactor cleanup — it's a different substrate for an entire class of problems currently considered intractable for electronics.
